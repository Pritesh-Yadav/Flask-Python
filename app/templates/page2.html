In 1962,Dr Leonard Kleinrock invented the queue structure for packets. It determined speed of the network through demand access and distributed control system. Paul Baran was hired to see if the circuit switching could be replaced with anything else. He got an idea from rat maze and came up with hot potato routing resulting in the invention of packet switching in 1969.
In 1966,Bob Taylor would have to log in different computers just to communicate. He wanted to build a system where the computers would be in a single network so that he would not need to log in into each and every terminal.The term that they used was RESOURCE SHARING. It was eventually used to transfer files. Taylor hired Dr.Lawrence Roberts to do the job as Roberts built the first connection between two computers at MIT before. Frank Hart from the BBN team bid the project on packet switching and built it.They also built amps for ARPANET. Their plan was to install computers with packet switching technology in four universities. The universities were:University of California, Los Angeles, (U.C.L.A.), the Stanford Research Institute (S.R.I.) , U.C. Santa Barbara (UCSB), and the University of Utah.
On September 1, 1969 first piece of hardware was installed at UCLA. By 1969, all the four computers were connected to each other. First files and data were sent amongst these computers but then eventually started sending email. Inventor of email was Raymond Tomlinson.


In 1972, ARPANET had fewer than 25 sites online. The National Science Foundation was on the network to receive grant money. In the absence of TCP/IP protocol there were different networks which would run with the help of their own set of rules instead of an unified one.This made the user experience really inconvenient. In 1992, there were only 50 website pages on the internet.

In 1993,Marc Andreessen at University of Illinois made the internet with GUI (Graphical User Interface) . For normal people it was very difficult to find whatever they wanted. Marc made a software which could show text and jump around a scrollbar making it user friendly for the users. After the advent of Marc's discovery, In 1993, the Internet grew by 341000%. At present there are 11 million domain names and 70 million websites.
