In world war II,circuit swithcing was used as the mode of communication. The drawback of this technology:it was a centralized system. Enemies could explode the central system and there would be no point of communication.So, leaders thought they needed another technology to avoid this. Therefore,President Dwight D Eisenhower started an organisation called ARPA(Advanced Research Project Agency). Objective of this organisation: To facilitate research in technology with potential military applications. ARPA gave their military requirements to private companies and universities to do the research on their behalf in exchange for money or funds. On February 7,1958 ARPA was renamed to DARPA(Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency). DARPA's first project was ARPANET(Advanced Research Projects Agency Network), which was the forerunner of the Internet in 1966.This project was initiated with J.C.R Licklider along with Bob Taylor. On August 6,1991,Al Gore,vice-president of the United States made ARPANET public. Internet was used by the military and organisation for research and development till that date. However, once it was made public people could use it to communicate to each other throughout the United States.
In 1989,Tim Berners-Lee, a British scientist,invented HTTP protocol and HTML for the publicly available internet. Before the advent of http protocol people could only send files and emails via the Internet but could not type in an address and view the web page present in that server.However, with the advent of the http protocol this was possible.
